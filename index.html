Conquer Theory and Practice Music

While conquering theoretical knowledge, music practice is also added to the classroom.

about me

I am from Xi'an, China, which is an ancient city with a long history. My music education experience started in my family. My mother likes to sing very much, and my family often holds small family concerts on weekends. Therefore, when I was a child at home, I would often hear my mother singing with full of emotion. From then on, I began to have some subtle interests in music under the influence of my mother. It was also from then on that the road of my music education experience started.

I started to learn piano with atomic analysis as a teaching method until I was 15 years old due to the reason of entering a higher school. In the teaching class during this period, my teacher taught in a step-by-step manner from simple to complex. For example, the starting exercise project is the practice of monophonic practice gradually changing to melody. At this stage, I feel indifferent to music and even a little bored. I am playful and don't like boring piano exercises that I think seem meaningless.

In a blink of an eye, when I was 18 years old, I came to a university class specializing in musicology. My major field of study is music history. Instructors in my college classes will primarily be lesson plan based, with formal project structures and final exams to complete. In the musicology class, university teachers will teach us knowledge about music history in a horizontal and vertical direction. The teacher will give a detailed list of the content we need to learn, and use the method of finally passing the exam to conduct the assessment. During the learning process, the teacher will clarify the music theory, for example, explain the musical forms that appeared in each historical period and the representative works of famous musicians in the form of noun explanation. In addition, this is a static and traditional subject, and the knowledge learned has existed for a long time, and it attaches great importance to ancient music culture.

After graduating from college, I majored in singing. My singing professional teacher's teaching emphasizes one-on-one lessons. Only then did I really have my own ideas about music. I realized that music is a long-term professional development, and it requires a lot of time to practice to make small progress.

My experience in Chinese music education made me realize that the content of music teaching in China is mainly preaching. I think this kind of teaching method made me not have my own ideas about music in college and before, but just followed the teacher's thinking. My music education experience was too rigid and only focused on the knowledge in the books, with too much emphasis on theory. In the process of learning, students do not have more room for thinking, just like an endorsement machine, they generally do not have their own references and opinions. For example: In the course of my undergraduate musicology study, there is no redundant learning content except for memorizing a large amount of Chinese and Western music development history and noun explanations. But this way of learning has allowed me to acquire more solid theoretical knowledge in the learning process. So I think China needs to constantly explore and learn from other cultures to complement each other. While conquering theoretical knowledge, music practice is also added to the classroom.
